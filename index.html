Phase 1: Exploring Digital Media


The Making 1

In this part of the project, I didn't really know a lot about coding, so I was really just focusing on taking stuff out and putting other stuff in. I wasn't super familiar with the style code either.

The Making 2

This second project was a lot easier, I could play more with colors and themes. And this time I understood more about how to tweak the style css. I also liked using my own photos and creating a photo gallery.

The Making 3

For this I was inspired by 2000s interfaces. If I had more time, I'd do something like this, but I also was trying to mimmick the simplicity of html style websites at the time that would lead to more complex.

Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

I really felt like this was a good balance between complex and artistry. I still have a lot to learn in terms of HTML, but I really enjoyed including my interests like photography and 2000s nastolgia.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Though I wasn't there for 2 of the project showings, but I really enjoy how they're worked into the class. It gives us as students some room for feedback and be able to workshop any problems we had before showing everyone. And it was so cool to see other people's pieces and use them for inspiration and be able to ask them what they did.

3. Think deeply about craft.

There was a lot to think about when puting this project together, so many different ideas that could be used, and so many different ways they could possibly be executed. I really just focused more on what things I liked and thought about how to execute them.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

I used a ton of stacked overflow and W3 to figure out how to do certain things. Some things I pulled off, but there is definitely more out there that I would like to learn to do. I think I definitely will keep working on website building, as I do really enjoy it. For my last project in particular, I used the internet archives to find older 2000s websites to link.

5. Learn to present your work

I like presenting my work in class and watching other people's work. I think over time it will help me to feel less stressed when giving presentations.

Phase 2/3 Course Goals

1. Foster the creative habits of mind

This phase was certainly harder than the last.It was a lot more skill building and learning than the first one, and some skills I'm still not great at. However it wasn't interesting to learn more apps in the adobe suite, I've only been experienced with maybe three of them. The projects were pretty interesting though and it was cool to see what other people turned in as well.


2. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Like I said before, it was cool seeing what other people had to show on class days. When I talked to some of the other people we were working in groups with and they agreed that some of the assignments were tougher in this phase, but a lot of people had really interesting projects to share.


3. Think deeply about craft

In phase 2, I think that I definitely was able to learn more skills that can apply to the final project, it gave me more things to think about.


4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

I really liked the research that we've been doing the last couple of weeks. I feel like it gave me a lot more structure in what I was doing for my project. It was nice to be able to organize my thoughts and explore different ideas. I really liked the article I read about wearable sculptures, it was very abstract but head great points to add to my discussion.


5. Learn to present your work

I don't really have anything to add from before. It's good practice to present our projects in class, it doesn't the other times I have to present in other classes less daunting.
